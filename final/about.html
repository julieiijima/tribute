1929

Born in Matsumoto, Nagano in Japan to a family of merchants.

1939

Starts painting at 10 years old to escape hallucinations, which included flowers speaking to her and flashes of light, auras, or dense fields of dots.

1950

Gains popularity in Japan as an artist. Infinity Nets, her first series or large-scale paintings were directly incorporated from her hallucinations.

1957

Moves to Seattle, U.S. where she held an exhibition for her paintings. After living there for a year, she moves to New York City and starts to build her reputation as an avante-garde movement leader. Many of her works include large paintings, soft sculptures, and environmental sculptures using mirrors and electrical lights, which are widely known as Infinity Rooms/Mirrors.

1960s

Conducts body-painting festivals, fashion shows, and anti-war demonstrations.

1973

Returns to Japan. While creating art for shows, she starts writing novels and anthologies.

1986 - 1993

Holds solo exhibitions across the globe: France, New York, MoMA, England, and much more.

1996

Mainly starts to show her work at galleries in New York. Her solo show in New York is named the Best Gallery Show from the International Association of Art Critics.

2004

Her solo exhibition KUSAMATRIX starts at Mori Museum in Tokyo. This exhibition drew visitors totaling 520,000 people. In the same year, another solo exhibition started at The National Museum of Modern Art, Tokyo

2006

Receives National Lifetime Achievement Awards, the Order of the Rising Sun, Gold Rays with Losette and The Praemium Imperiale -Painting- in 2006.

2008

Documentary film : Yayoi Kusama, I adore myself releases in Japan and also screens at the international film festival and museum.

2009

Solo exhibition at Gagosian Gallery NY and LA, Victora Miro Gallery in London and Padiglione d'Arte Contemporanea in Milan. Honored as Person of Cultural Merits in Japan.

2010

Solo exhibition and permanent outdoor sculpture at Towada Art Center in Japan and at Victoria Miro Gallery in London, fiac in Paris.

2011

Solo exhibition at Gagosian gallery (Roma), Victoria Miro Gallery (London). Europe and North America retrospective tour starts at Museo Nacional Centro De Arte Reina Sofia, Madrid traveling to Centre Pompidou (Paris), TATE MODERN (London) and Whitney Museum (New York). Solo exhibition at Watari Art Museum (Tokyo). Participates in the 2011 Chengdu Biennale (China). Programs solo exhibition at the Queensland Art Gallery (Brisbane) in November.

2012

“Eternity of Eternal Eternity", recent works solo national traveling show started at National Museum of Art, Osaka traveled to The Museum of Modern Art, Saitama, Matsumoto City Museum of Art, Nagano, Niigata City Art Museum. In the next year, it travelled to Shizuoka Prefectural Museum of Art, Oita Art Museum and the Museum of Art, Koch. Solo exhibition at Victoria.

- 2018

She continues to hold various shows and galleries at Museums all around the world, at age 89. Her work influences artists of all ages, including Andy Warhold, Carolee Schneemann, and Yoko Ono. Many describe her work as always being ahead of time, being the forefront of major artistic movements.
